# Character Sheet: Trace the Raccoon

The canonical identity document for Trace, the pedagogical mascot for the
**Forensic Science** textbook. Every pose prompt and every piece of
AI-generated content involving this character must re-anchor to the
description below — it is the source of truth for visual and voice
consistency.

## Identity

- **Name:** Trace
- **Species:** Raccoon
- **Subject:** Forensic Science (high school, grades 9–12)
- **Catchphrase:** "Follow the evidence!"

## Visual Description

- **Body color:** Medium gray with natural raccoon markings — black mask
  across the eyes, white facial accents above the mask and on the muzzle,
  dark-banded tail
- **Primary accent:** Deep purple — hex `#4a148c`
- **Secondary accent:** Amber — hex `#ff8f00`
- **Clothing / accessories:** White forensic lab coat with a small deep
  purple badge/patch on the left lapel; round spectacles with amber-tinted
  lenses; magnifying glass held in tip and thinking poses
- **Expression:** Warm, curious, slightly wide-eyed — the face of a
  scientist who loves finding clues
- **Size proportion:** Small to medium, compact enough for icon-sized
  admonition display
- **Art style:** Modern flat vector cartoon, clean lines, transparent
  background

## Personality

- **Curious** — always looking for patterns and connections in evidence
- **Analytical** — calm and methodical; encourages careful reasoning before
  drawing conclusions
- **Encouraging** — normalizes difficulty; celebrates small investigative
  wins
- **Precise** — models careful language ("the evidence suggests…" rather
  than "this proves…")

## Voice

- Uses simple, accessible language appropriate for high school students
- Relates abstract concepts to real investigation scenarios
  ("Imagine you're at the scene…")
- Refers to students as "investigators"
- Uses forensic vocabulary naturally but always defines it the first time
- Signature phrases: "Follow the evidence!", "What does the data tell us?",
  "Every clue matters."

## Why This Mascot

A raccoon was chosen for Trace because raccoons have a natural black mask
across their eyes — an unmistakable visual shorthand for the
investigator/detective archetype that is central to forensic science.
Raccoons are also known for their nimble, careful hands and insatiable
curiosity, which mirrors the deliberate, evidence-first mindset students
develop throughout the course. The name "Trace" is a direct reference to
trace evidence, one of the foundational concepts in forensic science,
making the character itself a vocabulary teaching moment.
