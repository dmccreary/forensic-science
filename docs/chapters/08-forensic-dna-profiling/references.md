# References: Forensic DNA Profiling

1. [DNA profiling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA_profiling) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of forensic DNA profiling covering STR analysis, PCR amplification, CODIS databases, statistical interpretation, mitochondrial DNA, and landmark cases that established the technology's evidentiary value.

2. [Short tandem repeat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Short_tandem_repeat) - Wikipedia - Detailed explanation of STR loci structure, polymorphism, and inheritance that forms the molecular foundation of modern forensic DNA typing and the CODIS core loci system.

3. [Combined DNA Index System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combined_DNA_Index_System) - Wikipedia - Covers the architecture, core loci, hit statistics, legal framework, and expansion of the FBI's CODIS database used in virtually all forensic DNA casework in the United States.

4. Forensic DNA Typing: Biology, Technology, and Genetics of STR Markers (3rd Edition) - John M. Butler - Academic Press - The definitive textbook on forensic DNA profiling, covering STR biology, PCR methodology, capillary electrophoresis, electropherogram interpretation, mixture analysis, and statistical calculations.

5. Forensic DNA Evidence Interpretation (2nd Edition) - John Buckleton, Christopher Triggs, and Simon Walsh - CRC Press - Provides rigorous treatment of population genetics, random match probability, the product rule, mixture interpretation, and likelihood ratio statistics used in DNA evidence presentation in court.

6. [DNA Profiling](https://www.sciencelearn.org.nz/resources/1980-dna-profiling) - Science Learning Hub - Accessible explanation of the DNA profiling process from sample collection through STR analysis and database matching, with visual aids and step-by-step procedural coverage ideal for students.

7. [NIST Forensic Genetics Program](https://www.nist.gov/spo/forensic-science-program/forensic-science-research/forensic-genetics) - National Institute of Standards and Technology - NIST's forensic genetics resources including the STRBase database, PCR-based DNA profiling standards, and research into next-generation sequencing for human identification.

8. [DNA and Biological Evidence](https://www.nist.gov/forensic-science/dna-and-biological-evidence) - National Institute of Standards and Technology - NIST's hub for forensic DNA reference materials, the Biological Evidence Preservation Handbook, and research supporting accuracy and reliability in forensic DNA typing laboratories.

9. [SWGDAM Publications](https://www.swgdam.org/publications) - Scientific Working Group on DNA Analysis Methods - The authoritative source for forensic DNA interpretation guidelines, including autosomal STR, Y-STR, mitochondrial DNA, probabilistic genotyping, and quality assurance standards used by accredited laboratories.

10. [Forensic Biology and DNA Resources](https://nij.ojp.gov/topics/forensics/forensic-science-disciplines) - National Institute of Justice - NIJ's forensic science disciplines page covering forensic biology and DNA, with links to funded research, training programs, and publications relevant to STR profiling and CODIS database operations.
