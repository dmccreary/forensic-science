# References: Facial Recognition Technologies and Biometric Identification

1. [Facial recognition system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Facial_recognition_system) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of facial recognition technology, including detection algorithms, landmark extraction, matching pipelines, law enforcement applications, and civil liberties debates. Essential foundation for this chapter's technical and legal content.

2. [Eigenface](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eigenface) - Wikipedia - Detailed explanation of the eigenface method using principal component analysis to reduce facial images to a low-dimensional feature space. Directly supports the chapter's coverage of foundational face recognition mathematics.

3. [Biometrics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biometrics) - Wikipedia - Covers the full spectrum of biometric identification systems, accuracy metrics (FAR/FRR), legal admissibility, and civil liberties issues, providing the broader context in which facial recognition evidence is evaluated in court.

4. Handbook of Biometrics - Anil K. Jain, Patrick Flynn, and Arun A. Ross - Springer - Authoritative reference covering biometric system design, face recognition pipelines, and performance evaluation; directly relevant to the eigenface, CNN, and error-rate topics in this chapter.

5. Handbook of Face Recognition (2nd Edition) - Stan Z. Li and Anil K. Jain (Eds.) - Springer - Comprehensive treatment of face detection, alignment, feature extraction, and recognition algorithms from 2D and 3D imagery; the standard academic reference for the technical methods covered in this chapter.

6. [Face Recognition Technology: EFF Analysis](https://sls.eff.org/technologies/face-recognition) - Electronic Frontier Foundation - Covers false-positive rates, documented wrongful arrests, demographic bias, and gaps in law enforcement oversight policies; directly relevant to the chapter's Daubert admissibility and algorithmic bias sections.

7. [ACLU: Face Recognition Technology](https://www.aclu.org/issues/privacy-technology/surveillance-technologies/face-recognition-technology) - American Civil Liberties Union - Documents civil liberties risks of facial recognition in mass surveillance, wrongful identification cases, and privacy concerns; supports the chapter's analysis of courtroom admissibility and demographic impact.

8. [NIST Biometrics Research and Standards](https://www.nist.gov/biometrics) - National Institute of Standards and Technology - NIST's biometrics program covering face recognition evaluation, age estimation, presentation attack detection, and algorithm accuracy measurement; the institutional source for FRVT performance benchmarks discussed in this chapter.

9. [EFF Surveillance Self-Defense](https://ssd.eff.org/) - Electronic Frontier Foundation - Practical guide to surveillance technology, biometric tracking risks, and digital privacy; provides accessible explanations of how facial recognition integrates with broader surveillance infrastructure relevant to CCTV analysis in this chapter.

10. [INTERPOL Digital Forensics](https://www.interpol.int/How-we-work/Innovation/Digital-forensics) - INTERPOL - Describes international standards for digital and biometric forensic evidence, expert networks, and guidelines for forensic laboratories; relevant to understanding how facial recognition evidence standards apply across jurisdictions.
