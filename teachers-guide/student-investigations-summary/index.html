    How Risk Is Rated¶

    The risk rating reflects the chance that a student or lab equipment could be harmed during the student-performed portion of the lab, assuming normal classroom PPE and supervision.

    Rating Meaning Examples in this book
    Low No hazardous chemicals, flame, or sharps in the student steps. Everyday or simulated materials. Tape lifts, chromatography, simulated blood typing, all Pure Virtual labs.
    Med Mild chemical irritants or sharp materials that need goggles, gloves, and close supervision. Kastle-Meyer (hydrogen peroxide), color spot tests (iodine), handling glass chips.
    High Open flame and/or flammable solvent — requires a fume hood or strong ventilation and one-station-at-a-time supervision. Fiber Under Fire (burn test + acetone).

    Always pair with your district's lab-safety policy

    These ratings are a planning aid, not a substitute for your own risk assessment. No investigation uses real blood, real controlled substances, or unsupervised flame — every "blood," "drug," and "accelerant" in the book is a safe simulant. Confirm PPE, ventilation, and disposal against your local requirements before running any physical lab.
